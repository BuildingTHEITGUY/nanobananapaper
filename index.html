Despite rapid advances in research methodologies at the University of Dubai, generating publication-ready illustrations remains a labor-intensive bottleneck in our research workflow. To address this challenge, we are implementing PaperBanana, an agentic framework for automated generation of publication-ready academic illustrations.

Powered by state-of-the-art Vision-Language Models and image generation technologies, PaperBanana will provide University of Dubai researchers with specialized agents to retrieve references, plan content and style, render images, and iteratively refine illustrations via self-critique.

This implementation directly supports our university's research excellence initiatives by significantly reducing the time researchers spend creating visualizations while enhancing the visual quality and consistency of our academic publications across all faculties.

Method Overview

The PaperBanana framework that we're implementing at University of Dubai utilizes a reference-driven agentic approach for automated academic illustration. As illustrated in the diagram below, the system orchestrates a collaborative team of five specialized agents—Retriever, Planner, Stylist, Visualizer, and Critic—to transform raw scientific content into publication-quality diagrams and plots.


  • Retriever Agent: Identifies relevant reference examples to guide downstream agents.
  • Planner Agent: Acts as the cognitive core, translating context into detailed textual descriptions.
  • Stylist Agent: Ensures adherence to University of Dubai's academic aesthetic standards by synthesizing guidelines from references.
  • Visualizer Agent: Transforms textual descriptions into visual output or executable code.
  • Critic Agent: Inspects generated images/plots against the source to provide feedback for refinement.

Benchmark Overview

PaperBanana's effectiveness is validated through rigorous benchmarking using PaperBananaBench, a dataset curated from NeurIPS 2025 methodology diagrams. This benchmark captures the sophisticated aesthetics and diverse logical compositions required for modern AI research papers, ensuring that our implementation at University of Dubai meets the highest standards of academic visualization.

Statistics of the test set of PaperBananaBench (totaling 292 samples). The average length of source context / figure caption is 3,020.1 / 70.4 words.

Performance Metrics

PaperBanana consistently outperforms leading alternatives across all four key evaluation dimensions: faithfulness, conciseness, readability, and aesthetics. These performance advantages will directly benefit University of Dubai researchers across all faculties.




The system also extends to the generation of high-quality statistical plots, an essential capability for quantitative research across our business, engineering, and science departments.

Key Applications for University Research

1. Enhancing Aesthetics of Existing Diagrams. Faculty can use PaperBanana to upgrade existing visualization materials, enhancing the visual appeal of current research without redoing work from scratch.


2. Precision Statistical Visualization. For our quantitative researchers, PaperBanana offers code-based plot generation that ensures 100% data fidelity while maintaining publication-quality aesthetics.

Implementation Plan at University of Dubai

Phase 1: Initial Setup (Q2 2026)

  • Infrastructure deployment on university cloud resources
  • Integration with existing academic workflow systems
  • Development of University of Dubai-specific style templates
  • Technical team training

Phase 2: Pilot Program (Q3 2026)

  • Deployment with Computer Science and Engineering departments
  • Faculty and graduate student training workshops
  • Collection of user feedback and system refinement
  • Preliminary assessment of impact on research productivity

Phase 3: University-wide Expansion (Q4 2026)

  • Extension to Business, Economics, and Natural Science departments
  • Integration with university publication workflows
  • Advanced training programs and resource development
  • Continuous assessment of research output metrics

Resource Requirements:
• Leverages existing university cloud infrastructure
• One technical administrator (part-time)
• Faculty champions in participating departments
• Minimal training resources (workshops and online materials)
